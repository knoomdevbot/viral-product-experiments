# UI guidelines: Friend-profile ShowDrop

Date: 2026-06-29 PDT

## Product feel

- Native US social/game copy, not “quiz platform” copy.
- The promise should be instantly legible: “put your friends inside the game.”
- Tone: affectionate chaos, not insult comedy.
- Outputs should feel like something made for a group chat or story post.

## Core UI principles

1. **The cast is the product.** Names, personas, and avatars should be visually prominent in the poster and role cards.
2. **Setup must feel like play.** Default templates and examples should make the creator laugh before typing anything.
3. **No creepy automation.** Manual entry is a feature for the first experiment; avoid suggesting scraping/importing people.
4. **Share before account.** No login, no onboarding wall, no account creation.
5. **Recipient understands they are included.** Shared links should make it obvious the game is about the group/personas, not a generic quiz.

## Share-card requirements for next slice

- 9:16 vertical format.
- Large title.
- 3–5 visible cast avatars or initials.
- One strong hook: “You’re in Episode 1.”
- CTA: “Claim your role” or “Make our version.”
- Avoid exposing sensitive raw text if shared publicly.

## Safety copy

Use:

- “Use nicknames, emoji, fictional personas, or photos your group is comfortable seeing.”
- “This is fictional role-play for friends.”

Avoid:

- “Import your friends.”
- “Analyze your group chat.”
- “Find out what your friends really think.”
